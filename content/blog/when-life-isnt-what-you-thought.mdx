"I think you'd look pretty cute pregnant," he said, glancing at me with a smile.

"I hope they get your blue eyes and blonde hair," I replied back looking at him with a smile as I reached for his hand.

"As long as they get your smile, I'd be happy," he added.

Dave and I were on the way to the doctor's office for my post op surgery results.

Four years earlier, at 27 and 31 years old, we married and were well into our careers, built our new house, and were ready to start a family. But, after two years, we still weren't pregnant. I suggested we head to the doctor. After weeks of the two of us being poked and prodded, the doctor performed laparoscopic surgery on me. After the surgery the doctore gave Dave great news that she was able to clean up the endometriosis in my lower abdomen increasing our chances of conceiving.

However, all our dreams, hope and optimism shattered as the doctor stated, "I don't know if you will ever conceive a child."

Those words would change the trajectory of my life for the next 6 years. I started down the path of questioning God's plan for my life as I fought for mental, emotional and spiritual health in my life as God asked me to trust Him for an unknown future.

Author and blogger Becky Graham shares her story of inconceivable trust in her newly released book, ***Faithful: An Unexpected Journey to Motherhood***.
