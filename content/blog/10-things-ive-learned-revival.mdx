There's revival in our area, one that's gaining national attention. 32oo people gather for a weeknight event. 52 days of prayer, community outreach, and sharing the gospel. 600 kids going into the community on a school day that's excused by public high schools.

Crazy.

Yet it's not. It's God at work through the ministry of [Revive Indiana](http://www.reviveindiana.org/). (You can learn more about it [here.)](http://www.reviveindiana.org/)

We've been involved. Lots to say, so here are ten things I've learned from revival.

1. Revival means the church *has to be revived* - which implies it's dead.

2. Revival starts in your own heart.

3. Humility brings healing.

4. When you expect God to work, He will.

5. Bold prayers in His will for His glory brings unexpected outcomes.

6. The Holy Spirit desires to work when you open the door and allow Him to work.

7. Unity is beautiful, exclusiveness is not.

8. The gospel is simple to share.

9. People are hungry for God.

10. God is bigger than fear.

I could expand on these, but each of them is big in themselves. Think through each one. Take it to the Lord, and ask Him how you can be a part of bringing His glory to your community.

I'm doing that through a [prayer initiative for public schools in Indiana,](https://www.facebook.com/events/1601117720120302/?pnref=story) of whom have been under attack for several years now.

I'm also asking God to provide in many ways. Will you join me? How do you need a fresh work of God in your life or circumstances?
