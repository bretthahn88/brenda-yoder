As my kids are making their homes in other places, home and the holidays are becoming [more intrinsic.](https://brendayoder.com/why-i-dont-hate-christmas-anymore/) Similar to the descriptions of home, home is a feeling more than anything.

That feeling is beautifully described in [Susie Finkbeiner's](https://susiefinkbeiner.com/) new book, [A Song of Home](https://www.amazon.com/Song-Home-Novel-Spence-Novels/dp/0825444829), a Christian novel, the [third in a series](https://susiefinkbeiner.com/pearlspencebooks/) which takes place during the Dust Bowl of the 1930's. One thing I love about the story is that it encompasses the feelings of home, but not in the Hallmark Christmas movie way. Through the story, you experience the pure and honest perception of what home is to a child who faces hardship, disappointment, and love.

What Home Means to Me
First, I'll share about home. Home is where the people I love the most are. It's where I can be myself, and where there's unconditional love. It's where I don't have to strive or perform. It's where I can be all of myself, the good, the bad, and the ugly, and not be judged. It's also a place what feels safe because of the atmosphere. Home is somewhere that's not so perfect, and yet simply beautiful and welcoming.
